Section 2

Executive Summary

A 1–2 page summary of your entire business plan. Write this last — but it appears first. Think of it as your plan’s highlight reel.

Business Overview

What do you do? Where are you located? What products or services do you sell?
Your background, skills, experience, or education that makes you the right person to run this business.

Key Business Information

What are your main goals? e.g. hire staff by Month 6, reach $80K revenue in Year 1, open a storefront by Year 2.
Who are your main customers? Who are your main competitors?
How will you reach customers and make sales?
If there are months before you earn revenue, what will you do to prepare and launch?

Financial Highlights

Do you need any special permits? What is the current status?
Section 3

Business Profile

Describe your business in detail — what it is, where it’s located, and how it’s organized.

Business Description

Is this new or existing? What type (retail, service, manufacturing)? Describe your products/services. If already operating, how many clients/sales so far? What is the planned start date?

Legal Structure

Business Location

Do customers come to you, or do you go to them? How can they contact you?

Business History & Relevant Dates

What have you already done? Registered the business, secured space, bought equipment, completed training?
Is the business year-round or seasonal? If seasonal, how will you earn revenue during slow periods?
Section 4

Management

Tell us about the people running the business and what experience they bring.

Owner / Management Profile

Include direct skills (baking, coding, accounting) AND transferable skills (customer service, project management, leadership). What makes you qualified?
If you have partners or key team members, list each person and what they bring.

Strategic Business Alliances & Support Network

List professionals and supporters who will help you. These show investors and lenders that you have a strong support team.
Section 5

Long Term Objectives

Where do you see your business in 3–5 years? Dream big and be specific.

Your 3–5 Year Vision

Where do you want your business to be in 3 to 5 years? Think about sales, income, employees, number of customers, and new products or services you plan to add.
What new offerings will you add to grow the business over time?
Section 6

Marketing Plan

Research your market, understand your customers, and plan how you will reach and attract them.

Industry Profile & Market Research

How big is your industry? Is it growing or declining? Key trends, opportunities, threats? Include NAICS code if you know it.
Consider: regulations/laws, economic conditions, social/cultural trends, technology changes.

Customer Segments

Who is your ideal customer? Demographics (age, gender, income, location) and psychographics (lifestyle, interests, values). Describe each segment separately if you have more than one.

Marketing Strategies – The 4 P’s

Where will you operate? Who goes to whom? Hours? Location advantages?
How did you determine your prices? How do they compare to competitors? Why higher/lower/the same?
How will you advertise? List all channels and activities: social media, events, word-of-mouth, flyers, website, etc.
Section 7

Sales Plan

Marketing creates prospects. Sales activities turn prospects into paying customers. Plan how you will actively pursue sales.

Sales Activities & Goals

Will you call them? Email? Meet at events? Social media DMs? Describe your step-by-step sales approach.
Do you need a sales script, pricing guide, proposal template, order form, contract?
How many customers/orders do you need per week or month to hit your revenue targets? How many leads will you contact each week?
Section 8

Operations Plan

Describe how your business works day-to-day — from making your product to delivering it to customers.

Operations Process

List the main steps from start to finish, with a short description and time estimate for each step.

Day-to-Day Operations

What does a typical business day look like? Production, sales, admin, customer service — and time spent on each.

Facilities

What space do you need? Size, type, setup, operating cost?

Equipment & Tools

What do you already have? What do you still need to buy or lease? What does it cost?

Materials & Supplies

What materials or supplies do you need? Where will you buy them? How will you track inventory?

Human Resources

Will you hire employees or contractors? When, how many, what skills, and what will you pay them?

Regulations & Insurance

Start-up Requirements

Start-up Costs

How much cash do you need to cover operating costs before revenue comes in?

Section 11

Risks & Critical Success Factors

Every business faces risks. Knowing them — and your plan to overcome them — shows lenders and advisors that you are prepared.

Risk Management

List each risk (industry, financial, or operational) and your mitigation strategy. Consider: slow sales, equipment failure, competition, regulatory changes, health issues.

Critical Success Factors

List the key reasons. Consider: your skills/credentials, niche positioning, branding, key partnerships, customer relationships, technology, industry associations.
Section 12

Financial Plan

Describe your financial forecasts in your own words. Your financial spreadsheets (Excel/Google Sheets) are kept separately — use this section to explain your numbers and assumptions.

📊 Note: Your actual financial spreadsheets (Sales Forecast, Cash Flow, Income Statement) are provided separately by your PowerUp advisor. Use the text boxes below to write the narrative explanations and assumptions behind your financial projections.

Start-up Funding Plan

Personal savings, loan, grant? When will you secure funding? What are the loan repayment terms? What will the money be used for?

Sales Forecast Assumptions

How did you calculate your Year 1 revenue? Explain your assumptions: number of clients, average order value, revenue streams, seasonal patterns.
How will Year 2 differ from Year 1? Growth expectations and why?

Cash Flow & Income Statement Notes

When does money come in vs. go out? Large expenses, seasonal cash gaps?
Expected profit/loss Year 1 & 2? When do you break even?
